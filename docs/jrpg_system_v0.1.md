# [WORKING TITLE] — Core System v0.1

*A deep, party-based JRPG. Turn-based, math-heavy, built around soul-bound weapons that amplify off the wielder's spirit. Old legends sit in the backdrop and become tap-into-able toward the endgame.*

> **Placeholder names are marked in [brackets]** — still-open naming. Established canon is in §0.

---

## 0. World Canon (established)

- **Resonance Weapon** — a weapon that amplifies off its wielder's spirit. Colloquially a **"Reason."** (The pun is load-bearing: your Reason is both the weapon you carry and the thing you'd die to protect.)
- **Resonant** — a person able to project their spirit far enough to touch and activate a Reason. **Rare and not trainable** — you have the spark or you don't.
- **Compatibility matters.** Anyone with enough spirit *can* wield any Reason, but the pairing may be poor. A Reason isn't universally "good" — it's good *for a particular soul*. Finding your Reason = finding the one that fits your spirit.
- **Manifestation is wielder-specific.** The same Reason blooms differently on different Resonants — the power comes *through the person*, not just out of the blade (e.g. a dark Reason growing large dark wings that let its wielder fly).
- **Anoridami Resonant Academy** — the state institution that credentials Resonants, run by the **Anoridami Government**. Admission is regarded as an amazing honor and a stroke of luck. **But its prestige is a fragile fiction:** everyone knows Resonants are *born, not made* — the Academy can't produce the gift, only credential and polish it. It gatekeeps something it has no power to create.
- **Caught between classes.** Because being a Resonant is seen as luck rather than merit, a **low-born** Resonant is squeezed from both sides: the poor resent him for rising above them, and the rich look down on him for lacking breeding. He belongs to neither world. *(This rootlessness is the emotional engine behind the party / found-family — the belonging the world refuses him.)*
- **Setting — Anoridami, a futuristic city-state.** Not medieval fantasy: a sleek, modern city-state where spirit-driven legendary weapons and advanced technology coexist. The aesthetic tension (chrome over old blood) mirrors the thematic one — a polished present built on a wild, buried past. There is an ongoing **war effort**, and **conscription falls on the lower class** — the poor are drafted and "lost" while the aristocracy is spared. (The war's true nature/enemy is an open thread; it is also the engine of Siren's personal grief — see Cast doc.)
- **The Resonant Temple — a facade, and a prison.** The holiest site in Anoridami is a lie: a vast temple built *around* the original village where **the First Resonant** was born, specifically to keep everyone out. It isn't a tomb — it's a **quarantine around a living woman the government could not defeat.** Inside is **Karatsul**: over 300 years old, appears in her 30s, sustained by a **Perfect Alignment to the village itself** (§9 — an abomination in the government's eyes). **She was not one of the six founders** — originally a **healer** (though she wields an extremely powerful Reason), later a leader in the *first* government, which she abandoned when she saw greed and avarice creep in. She refused to surrender her secrets, and **no one could beat her in the village.** So the state walled the whole site off and pretends it's a shrine. Every pilgrim prays at the outer wall of the state's living conscience. **She sealed herself behind a door that opens only for a Pure Resonant with the potential for Primal (§9)** — i.e., only for someone who could have been her son. The lock *is* her vigil. *(That the deepest root of all this weaponized power is a **healer** and her son defending home means Resonance's true nature is care and protection — and the saints' weaponizing of it is a perversion of what it is.)*
- **The origin myth — Primal Resonance.** Anciently, Resonance was **common and spiritual** — communion with the divine *or* the evil — and **Pure Resonance was a known, even required thing** (see World Lore). What was *unprecedented* belonged to **Karatsul's son** — born mute and dismissed as a special-needs child, until his power ignited defending the village. Raw, whole emotion became **Primal Resonance**: the first ever, **self-reached**, *instinctual and able to reshape the physical world* (he could broadcast **Silence** across a whole place). He is **the First Resonant** the state now venerates as an icon — while the truth is buried: he was **captured and taken for experiments** by the powers that became today's government. **The experiments' purpose: to reverse-engineer the spark and *manufacture* Resonance — and, it turned out, to *mass-produce Reasons* too** (World Lore) — the origin of the Artificial program and the state's whole synthetic war machine. Karatsul kept his Reason. *The state worships a boy it cut open to mass-produce what he was.*
- **The village is the gate to real knowledge.** Sealed inside are the writings on the First Resonant and the deep art of **Alignment and True Alignment** — knowledge the Academy credentials people around but does not itself possess. This makes the Academy *doubly hollow* (can't create the gift, doesn't hold the art) and makes reaching the true village the story's hidden goal.
- **Tech is fused with Resonance — and Resonants are keys.** Anoridami's technology channels and amplifies spirit, but because Resonants are rare, **certain doors, systems, and vaults can only be opened by a Resonant.** They aren't just warriors; they're living keys. This is the real reason the government must credential and leash every one through the Academy — *access control*, dressed as honor. A rare Pure Resonant with a legendary familial Reason may open doors even the government cannot.
- **Artificial Resonants — the state's manufactured spark.** Descended directly from the experiments on the First Resonant, the Government has learned to *manufacture* Resonants. **They aren't named anything special — everyone is just called a "Resonant"** — and that blur is the point: **most Resonants in the Academy and on the frontlines are Artificial or very weak true ones.** Genuine, *powerful* true Resonants are rare. Artificials can be immensely powerful, but their bond to their Reason is **engineered, not true** — so they're **capped below real alignment** (no Pure/Perfect/Primal) and **catastrophically vulnerable to Silence**, which strips a wielder down to only the true bond they don't have. The **war effort feeds the program** — the conscripted poor as raw material — reframing Siren being "rejected for physical attributes" as *rejected as unsuitable stock.* Because real power is so scarce, **Siren is coveted, not just scorned**: a Pure true Resonant of his strength is a *tool* if the right faction gets its hooks in — so he walks into the Academy sneered at by (often artificial) highborn students and quietly hunted by everyone above them. **The Iron Hand Saint is Artificial** (the program's masterpiece); **the Public Face Saint is a rare *true*, powerful Resonant of real original-family blood** — Siren's foil (Cast doc).
- **The Founding & the Split.** The kingdom was founded by **six heroes** (six families). Then it fractured:
  - **Three families** chose wealth, power, and the *weaponizing* of Resonance → they became today's corrupt **Anoridami Government** and its aristocracy. In official lore they are **"the three saints"** who helped the Primal Resonant defeat the invaders — propaganda laid directly over the crime, since those same three seized the boy the moment the invaders were gone. Today they run a **three-party "democracy" that is a sham** — the three parties perform rivalry for the public but are **secretly unified.** (The political lie and the buried atrocity are the same lie at two scales; there is no "good party" to back.) The **current three saints aren't literal descendants** — they *assume* the family names and mantles, so the houses that rule by the sacred authority of *lineage* aren't even real lineages; they're titles worn by whoever holds power. Three distinct antagonist faces fronting one bloc.
  - **Two families** refused and **left entirely for the heathen provinces** outside the kingdom, where the old ways survive (see below).
  - **One family — the protagonist's** — stayed and tried to keep the founding's intention pure. They had always been kind to Karatsul and her son, and they **tried to free the boy from the experiments and failed.** Refusing to bend, they left the leadership — and were ground into **generational poverty and ostracism.** The MC is the heir of the founders who *paid* for their conscience.
- **🔩 The founding war & the foreign power (open thread).** Official lore says the three saints helped the Primal Resonant **defeat invaders** at the founding — but *who* were the six families fighting? There is a **foreign country/power** (Siren fights them on an Academy mission), and the current **war effort** is aimed outward at it. Open and rich: was the founding "invasion" real, provoked, or a lie? Is the foreign enemy a genuine threat, a manufactured one to justify the draft/Artificial program, or a people as wronged as the heathens? This is where the map expands beyond Anoridami — designed later, but flagged as a major vein.
- **Everyone has Primal potential — it just has to be *unlocked*.** Primal Resonance (§9) isn't a rare birthright; the latent capacity is universal. It must be awakened, and **Karatsul is the one who can do it** — for *almost anyone*. But she gives it **only to the pure**, and so for 300 years she has given it to almost no one; she **withheld it from most of the original founders**, including the three who became saints, because she saw what they would do with it. Her vigil is also a *guard* — she is the single reason the corrupt three never obtained the ultimate power. The MC's Primal potential is dormant and unknown; he gains it later, from her, because she judges him pure. *(Theme-bomb: the ruling three built their hierarchy on the scarcity of Resonance — but the deepest power was never scarce, only withheld from the impure. Returning that truth dissolves the ruling class's reason to exist.)*
- **The heathen provinces & the Da'Charta.** Outside the kingdom live an **indigenous people** — proud keepers of the **old, pre-Primal form of Resonance** the state mostly erased and dismisses as "heathen." (Touchstone for the *dynamic*, à la Native American nations vs. an expansionist state: a living, dignified tradition marginalized by a "civilized" kingdom — not primitives.) The **two exiled founding families merged with them into one tribe, the Da'Charta.** They are **at peace and not bitter;** they simply keep the old ways. The old resonance is **with spirit itself** (the Academy calls it "Abnormal") — communal, not weaponized; the truer root the empire lost. Act 2 out here is grounded and personal: the MC gains one meaningful ally — **Katariña, a Da'Charta warrior of founding descent** (kin to Siren across the divide, carrying the ancestral **Staff of Nagandahl**) — and learns the older way, rather than rallying a legion. **Da'Charta prophecy foretells the *redemption of Resonance* and a coming stranger;** when Siren arrives they welcome him without protest because they *feel* his purity on sight — the truth the empire is blind to. The prophecy also named **Katariña at her birth** as the one who would **love the next Primal Resonant (and he her)** — her existence a marker the time was near, and (on meeting Siren) the sign that tips them to shelter his mother. **Katariña is Siren's foretold love** (a slow burn); in the Pure ending he settles among the Da'Charta with her.
- **The endgame.** The MC eventually wields **both his ancestor's Reason *and* Karatsul's son's Reason**, perfectly aligned, to bring down the corrupt government — the founding's two purest strands (the loyal family + the origin itself) reuniting to finish what the Split interrupted.
- **Core theme.** The institution *forgot its own root* — and then buried it, tortured it, and branded the people who kept it "heathens." The misfit protagonist — low-born, carrying his family's **legendary heirloom Reason**, and holding an unheard-of **Pure Alignment (§6, Tier 3)** — is that root returning. He's *told* he's Pure but doesn't grasp what it means (the player learns alongside him). His family holds only **faded old rumors** of who they once were — legends worn down by generations of poverty, half-believed and treated as not mattering anymore — so the truth lands less as fresh revelation than as *the old family stories turning out to be real.*

## 1. Design Pillars

Every mechanic below serves one of these. If a future addition doesn't, it's probably wrong for this game.

1. **The weapon is the spectacle.** Not a stat stick — a partner. The "big moment" is a weapon awakening and channeling the wielder's spirit, not a generic limit break.
2. **Same weapon, different soul, different beast.** Weapon abilities scale off the *wielder's* stats, so gear expresses character rather than replacing it.
3. **Weaknesses are loud.** Hitting an element weakness is a screen-lighting event that also *feeds* the weapon. Combat rewards reading the enemy.
4. **Two progression tracks.** You grow the character (levels) *and* the bond with the weapon (attunement). Both matter; neither alone is enough.
5. **Legends are earned, not given.** Mythology is texture for most of the game, then becomes literal power near the end.

## 2. Core Stats

Every combatant (party and enemy) uses this block.

| Stat | Name | Governs |
|------|------|---------|
| **VIT** | Vitality | Max HP |
| **PWR** | Power | Physical damage dealt |
| **FOC** | Focus | Arte (magic) damage dealt |
| **SPR** | **Spirit** | **The soul stat.** Weapon Resonance gain, Amplified arte power, Form access |
| **GRD** | Guard | Physical damage reduction |
| **WRD** | Ward | Arte damage reduction |
| **AGI** | Agility | Turn speed (how often you act) |
| **LCK** | Luck | Crit rate, status landing/resisting |

**SPR is the keystone.** It does triple duty — it fills the weapon gauge faster, it amplifies signature moves, and it gates which weapon Forms a character can reach. A high-SPR character isn't just "magical"; they're the one who makes weapons sing.

**Resources**
- **HP** — derived: `MaxHP = 40 + VIT * 8 + (level * 6)`
- **MP** — spent on artes: `MaxMP = 10 + FOC * 2 + (level * 1)`
- **Resonance** — the weapon gauge (0–100), see §6. This is the star.

## 3. Turn System — [CTB / "Tick Order"]

Conditional turn-based, tick-driven (think FFX / Grandia). Cleanest for both deep math *and* a future AI engine to compute a visible turn queue.

Each actor carries a **tick counter**. Lowest counter acts next. After acting, their next turn is pushed forward:

```
next_turn = now + round( BASE_TICK / AGI * action_weight )

BASE_TICK = 300 (global tuning constant)
action_weight:  fast action = 0.7   |   normal = 1.0   |   slow/heavy = 1.5
```

**Worked example**
- AGI 30, normal action → 300/30 × 1.0 = **10 ticks** until next turn
- AGI 60, normal action → 300/60 × 1.0 = **5 ticks** (acts twice as often)
- AGI 30, heavy arte → 300/30 × 1.5 = **15 ticks** (big moves cost tempo)

This makes AGI genuinely valuable (fast characters get more actions, not just first strike) and makes heavy signature moves a real tempo decision. The turn queue is always displayable as an ordered list — good for UI later.

**Party size:** 4 active + a reserve bench. Swapping in a reserve costs a normal-weight action. (Bench characters gain reduced XP — keeps everyone viable.)

**No rows.** Everyone fights on a single line — positioning is not a layer. Combat stays about turn order, elements, and Resonance, not geometry.

## 4. Damage Formulas

Multiplicative mitigation (never goes negative, scales smoothly, lets weaknesses feel *huge*).

**Physical**
```
dmg = (PWR + weapon_atk) * (skill_power / 100)
        * affinity * (100 / (100 + GRD)) * variance * crit
```

**Arte (magic)**
```
dmg = (FOC + arte_bonus) * (skill_power / 100)
        * affinity * (100 / (100 + WRD)) * variance * crit
```

- `skill_power` — 100 for a basic attack; artes/skills scale from ~60 (cheap) to ~250+ (signature)
- `affinity` — see §5 (this is the flashy multiplier)
- `variance` — random 0.95–1.05
- `crit` — 1.5× on crit; crit chance = `5% + LCK/2 %`

**Sanity check (early game, ~level 10):** PWR 45, weapon_atk 30, enemy GRD 40, basic attack.
`= 75 × 1.0 × 1.0 × (100/140) ≈ 54 dmg` vs ~400 HP → ~7 hits to kill. Good baseline.
On a **weakness** (2.0×): `≈ 108` → the number roughly doubles and the screen should *react*.

## 5. Elements & Affinity — "The Flashy Lights"

Six elements. Because Resonance runs on emotion, each element is both a physical domain and an **emotional/spiritual domain** — an element isn't assigned to a Resonant so much as it *expresses what their spirit reaches toward.*

| Element | Is | Represents | Plays as |
|---------|-----|-----------|----------|
| **Ember** | fire, heat, combustion | **Will, defiance** — passion & wrath; resonating toward *wanting* | Aggression; **Burn** (DoT + softens GRD) |
| **Tide** | water, ice, flood | **Emotion, memory, grief** — what flows and drowns; resonating toward *what's lost* | Control; **Freeze** (Stagger, push enemy turn back) |
| **Gale** | wind, storm, lightning | **Freedom, thought, restlessness** — breath & change; resonating toward *escape/becoming* | Speed & multi-hit; the flashy lightning |
| **Stone** | earth, rock, metal | **Endurance, home, protection** — things that stay; resonating toward *what you'd shield* | Defense; **Guard Break**; heavy single blows |
| **Radiance** | light, sun, revelation | **Truth, exposure** — hope, judgment, dragging secrets into the open; resonating toward *what should be seen* | Marks/exposes enemies (easier to Rupture); some healing |
| **Umbra** | shadow, void, the buried | **The hidden, the unknown** — secrets, grief's dark half; resonating toward *what's been denied* | **Seal, Weaken**, life-drain |

**The wheel — three opposed pairs** (each pair is also a thematic argument, handy for boss design):
- **Ember ⚔ Tide** — will vs. grief
- **Gale ⚔ Stone** — freedom vs. rootedness
- **Radiance ⚔ Umbra** — the exposed vs. the hidden

**Affinity multipliers**
| Result | When | Multiplier | Effect |
|--------|------|-----------|--------|
| **Rupture** | hit with the element *opposite* the target's affinity | **2.0×** | Bonus damage **+ big Resonance gain** (§6) |
| Neutral | any non-paired element | 1.0× | — |
| Resist | hit with the target's *own* element | 0.5× | Chip damage, minimal gauge |
| Null / Absorb | special enemies/bosses only | 0× / heal | Punishes blind casting |

**The key link:** a Rupture doesn't just deal more — it *floods the Resonance gauge*. Reading weaknesses is how you power signature moves. Combat literacy and spectacle are the same system.

*Emotional-expression hook:* a Resonant's element mirrors a **core note of their spirit — but not their whole personality**, and it often surfaces *obliquely* rather than on the surface. (A **Stone** type might read as a lone wolf yet be fiercely loyal to someone already gone; a **Tide** type may seem steady and dry while grief runs underneath.) Assigning a party member an element is really assigning them a buried inner truth. Known so far: the **MC is Tide** (loss, memory — and the same element as Karatsul, which is why their final meeting lands); the dark-winged flier is **Umbra**.

*Later:* each element may branch into **refined sub-types** (e.g. Tide → deep/ice/flood variants) as a mid-game expansion — reserved, not built yet.

*Dual elements (with a guardrail).* A Resonant's **native element is their soul** — one, permanent, expressive; it never changes. A **second** element can come two ways, without diluting that: **(a) Awakened** — a *true* second facet of the soul surfacing, rare and story/high-level driven (e.g. **Cinne** Gale→Umbra; **Mael** Tide→Gale, "the tempest" — born to the water, grew the storm as he broke free); only some ever do this. **(b) Borrowed** — equip an **off-native Reason** and you can channel *its* element while wielding it, but it's a *tool, not your soul*: weaker, and compatibility-gated (ties into the Reason-swap/Attunement system, §6c/§7). So native = identity, awakened = earned growth, borrowed = just a different weapon.

*🔒 The seventh element — **Silence.*** A type **outside the six**, manifested once in history by the First Resonant at his self-reached Primal (§9). **Silence doesn't attack — it *isolates.*** It removes a target from their element *and* their power, stripping away everything external until only the bond between Resonant and Reason remains — then tests that bond at the deepest level possible. A **false or shallow bond collapses** in the silence (nothing was really there); a **true bond endures.** It is the power of total abandonment, from the boy who knew nothing but abandonment his whole life — structurally, the "even the heavens go quiet" test of faith. It is also the literal question Siren's Perfect Alignment ("What Survives," Cast doc) is named to answer: strip away everything, and what's left? Unique, never seen before or since; returns to the world only when Siren takes up the son's Reason at the endgame — where it becomes the finale's win condition, since the **hollow name-bearer saints cannot survive Silence** and Siren, forged in loss, can. *(Mechanics reserved.)*

## 6. ⭐ The Weapon System — Reasons ⭐

**This is the game.** Everything else exists to make this shine.

A **Reason** (formally, Resonance Weapon) is wielded by a **Resonant** who can project spirit into it. It has base stats *and* three things stat-sticks don't. **Manifestations bloom on the wielder, not just the blade** — the power comes *through the person*.

**Alignment is a ladder measuring the *depth and purity of the connection* between Resonant and Reason** — not a divine or moral scale, but the *nature of the bond itself.* It doubles as the world's status hierarchy, the progression spine, and the endgame divergence. Full writeup in §9; summary:

| Tier | State | What it means | Rarity |
|------|-------|---------------|--------|
| **1 — Awakening** | Temporary combat burst (§6b) | You *reach* the Reason; it flares and fades | Every Resonant |
| **2 — True Alignment** | Near-permanent mastery | The Reason *reaches back and stays* (the dark-winged flier) | Elite. *The deep art is sealed in the village.* |
| **3 — the Fusion fork** | **Pure** ⟺ **Impure** Alignment | Total fusion — forked by the *purity of the connection* | **Basically unheard of.** Protagonist has **Pure** |
| **4 — Perfect Alignment** | Flawless, complete bond | A total connection; transcendent (Karatsul → the village, 300+ yrs) | The rarest documented state |
| **5 — Primal Resonance** | The origin-state | The rawest, purest connection — *pure emotion toward a goal or loved one* | Legendary. Karatsul's son, the First Resonant |

- **The Fusion fork (Tier 3) — purity of connection.** **Pure** = a whole, undivided bond (the protagonist): the **strongest** fusion there is, but the **hardest to obtain.** **Impure** = the same depth reached *mixed* — through corruption, hunger, or sacrifice. It is **weaker than Pure**, just far **easier and faster** to reach — and it still *costs* the wielder, bleeding into and corrupting them. So Impure is never an optimization; it's a **compromise that costs twice** (a lesser ceiling *and* it rots you). Not evil — just a settling.
- **Primal is neutral.** It is *emotion toward a goal or loved one*, nothing more — protective in the son's case, but the same raw connection lit by vengeance or obsession would be its mirror. This is where "what do you become" lives: not good vs. evil, but *what you resonate toward, and how cleanly.*

**Pure Alignment is the protagonist's edge and his curse** — the top of the *known* world, which is why it leapfrogs him past highborn students. But it's only the doorway to what's above.

### 6a. Resonance (the gauge, 0–100)
Fills from three sources:
```
per turn:      + SPR / 10           (passive attunement — high spirit = faster fill)
on hit dealt:  + 4
on Rupture:    + 15                 (weaknesses supercharge the weapon)
on hit taken:  + 6                  (adversity feeds it too)
```
So a high-SPR wielder who reads weaknesses charges *dramatically* faster than a brute swinging blind. Spirit literally powers the weapon.

**The gauge takes a personal *form* per Resonant (on-theme).** Resonance amplifies off the individual, so the *same underlying gauge wears a different face in each soul* — one universal grammar (a 0–100 bar that fuels Awakening + Amplify), but unique fill rules and quirks per character. This gives each party member a signature resource without fragmenting combat:
- **Siren — Resonance** (the default): steady, spirit-fed, *chosen* release. Reliable.
- **Cinne — Rage:** fills from *violence* (landing crits, taking hits) and **isn't spent so much as *survived.*** At max it **seizes her** — she drops into a bloodthirsty, sadistic state whether she wants to or not: huge crit output but she loses control (indiscriminate targeting, can't be reined in) for a few turns. Her loop *is* her arc — vent it before it crests and stay herself, or let it max and become what she fears. "Master it or be mastered," as a bar you watch all fight. (A live **Impure** risk.)
- **Katariña — Fervor:** her old-ways form (§ Cast doc) — built by *enduring*, spent on totemic invocations rather than a weapon Awakening.
- **Earl — Attunement** is his personal quirk atop a standard gauge (the manufactured boy assigning himself an element he doesn't have).

### 6b. Awakening — Tier I (the temporary manifestation)
At **Resonance 100**, spend it to **Awaken** for **3 turns**:
- weapon_atk gains a large multiplier (e.g. ×1.5)
- unlocks Awakened-only artes
- a partial manifestation flares on the wielder's body — **the screen-lights-up moment**

Which manifestation a Resonant can reach is gated by their **SPR** and their Attunement with *that specific Reason* (§7) — since compatibility varies, the same Reason awakens grander on a well-matched soul and weakly on a poor pairing. A Resonant who pushes attunement far enough crosses into **Tier II — True Alignment**, where the manifestation stops fading between fights and becomes a standing feature of the character (dark wings they simply *have* now). *Same Reason, different soul, different beast* — mechanically enforced.

**Cost decay (progression payoff).** Awakening costs a full gauge (100) at first, but as Attunement climbs the cost *drops* — and at high mastery a bond can grow so strong that **Awakening becomes free**, invoked at will without spending Resonance (which then flows entirely into Amplified Artes). This is the mechanical bridge toward True/Perfect Alignment, where the manifestation is essentially always available.

### 6c. Amplified Artes (the signature move)
Instead of Awakening, you can dump Resonance into a single **Amplified Arte**. Its power scales off the wielder:
```
amplified_skill_power = base_skill_power + (SPR * amp_coefficient)
```
Every weapon has its own `amp_coefficient` and its own governing stat flavor. A weapon on a SPR-heavy healer becomes a totally different tool than the same weapon on a SPR-light striker.

**Amplifies are emergent from the *pairing*, not the weapon alone.** A Reason contributes an element + a manifestation; a wielder contributes stats + spirit + their emotional element; the **specific Amplified-arte set is unique to that (character × Reason) combination.** Put Hymerdom in Cinne's hands and it generates *different* Amplifies than Hymerdom in Siren's — because an Amplify is the fusion of who they are with what the weapon is. This makes weapon-swapping a real system: any Resonant can attune to any Reason (compatibility varies — poor pairings give weak, generic Amplifies; **true pairings**, like Siren + his ancestral Hymerdom, give the strongest, most bespoke sets). Design handling: each character has a **native Reason** with a full, hand-authored Amplify set; off-pairings draw from a smaller emergent set so the combinatorics stay manageable.

**Worked example:** base signature arte skill_power 250, wielder SPR 50, amp_coefficient 2.0
→ amplified_skill_power = 250 + 100 = **350**
→ physical dmg ≈ `75 × 3.5 × (100/140) ≈ 187` (neutral) / **~375 on a Rupture** — a genuine half-a-healthbar, lean-forward hit.

**The core decision loop every fight:** *bank Resonance toward an Awakening, or spend it now on one huge Amplified strike?* Tempo vs. burst, mediated by Spirit.

## 7. Progression — Two Tracks

**Track A — Character Level.** XP from battles. Each level applies per-character growth curves to the 8 stats (every character has a stat "personality" — one is a SPR monster, one is a PWR bruiser, etc.). Standard, computable, the reliable spine.

**Track B — Weapon Attunement Rank.** Weapons level *through use* (actions taken while equipped). Rising Attunement Rank unlocks:
- higher Ascendant Forms
- additional Amplified artes
- passive bonuses that scale off the wielder's SPR

This means a beloved weapon *grows with its wielder* — and re-attuning a weapon to a new character is a real cost, not a free swap. Bonds matter.

## 8. Status Effects (starter set)

Poison (DoT), Burn (DoT + reduces GRD), Stagger (pushes tick counter back — tempo denial), Seal (blocks artes), Weaken (−PWR/FOC), Guard Break (−GRD/WRD). Landing chance modified by attacker LCK vs. defender LCK. Expand later.

## 9. The Alignment Ladder (full) — 🔒 Tier 4+ reserved

*The complete progression + status hierarchy + endgame divergence in one structure. The axis is the **depth and purity of the connection** between Resonant and Reason — not divinity, not morality. Tiers 1–3 are playable; Tier 4+ is mythical, tap-into-able only toward the end.*

**Tier 1 — Awakening.** Temporary manifestation (the Resonance-100 burst, §6b). Every Resonant. You reach the Reason; it flares and fades.

**Tier 2 — True Alignment.** The manifestation becomes permanent — the Reason reaches back and stays (the dark-winged flier). Elite students reach this. *The deep art of how it actually works is sealed in the village* — the Academy trains toward it without holding its real knowledge.

**Tier 3 — the Fusion fork (Pure ⟺ Impure).** You and the Reason become *one*. Same depth of fusion; forked by the *purity of the connection*:
- **Pure Alignment** — a whole, undivided bond. The protagonist. Clean, effortless once reached, always available — and the **strongest** fusion there is, but the **hardest to obtain.**
- **Impure Alignment** — the same oneness, but *mixed* — reached through corruption, hunger, or sacrifice. **Weaker than Pure**, just far **easier and faster** to reach, and it still bleeds into the wielder and changes them. The tempting *shortcut*, never an upgrade.

**Impure Resonant vs. Artificial Resonant — the key distinction.** An **Artificial** bond is *engineered and empty* — nothing underneath, which is why **Silence annihilates it.** An **Impure** bond is *genuine and deep, but corrupted*: real fusion reached through a tainted emotional core — love curdled to obsession, grief to vengeance, devotion to fanaticism, or fused via self-erasure and sacrifice. Because the connection is *real*, **Impure survives Silence** (there IS something underneath — it's just dark). But it **feeds on the wielder** — power bought by trading away pieces of the self; it warps body and mind over time. It is a **compromise, never an optimization** — *weaker* than Pure, taken only because Pure was out of reach or its price too steep. It can be a **shortcut** (easier than Pure) or a **fall** (a Pure heart that curdles). It is **Siren's shadow-path**: a Tide Resonant whose grief hardens into vengeance Impure-aligns — settling for a lesser, self-consuming power. The whole endgame divergence (Pure Primal vs. Impure Primal) turns on whether his loss stays love or becomes wrath. The **Keeper Saint** (if Impure) is a living preview of what Siren could become.

**Tier 4 — Perfect Alignment.** A flawless, total connection — and, like Primal, **morally neutral: it measures the *completeness* of the bond, not its goodness.** **Karatsul** holds a Perfect Alignment to the *village* (aligned to something she loves), which has sustained her body for 300+ years — near-immortality. But a flawless bond can be aligned to something evil: **some Reasons have a genuinely malevolent *nature*** (not neutral instruments — *evil Reasons*), and a dark soul in Perfect Alignment with one shares a single seamless will with it. The **Public Face Saint** is exactly this — a **dark Perfect**: real bond (so **Silence does nothing**), yet flawless and stable (unlike self-consuming **Impure**). Karatsul's exact mirror, and the story's apex villain. **Siren's own Perfect is "What Survives"** (Cast doc) — which is *not enough* to beat a Perfect-evil; he must reach **Primal** (Tier 5, the son's Silence Reason) to surpass it. That's why the endgame needs Karatsul's gift.

**Tier 5 — Primal Resonance (the origin-state).** The rawest, purest connection possible: **pure emotion toward a goal or a loved one.** What sets it apart from a merely deep Pure bond: **Primal is *instinctual* and *interacts with the physical world.*** Where Pure/Impure work *through* the wielder and their Reason, Primal **reaches out and rewrites reality on instinct** — Karatsul's son could **broadcast Silence across a whole place**, isolating everyone from their power at once. (This is why the state was desperate to dissect him: not a strong swordsman, but a boy who could unmake a battlefield by *feeling.*) Anciently, **Pure Resonance was common** (chieftaincy required it); the son's feat was the **first *Primal* ever** — self-reached, unprecedented, and it manifested a **wholly new element, Silence** (§5).

**The potential for Primal is universal but dormant** — everyone else has it, but it must be *unlocked*, and **Karatsul can do it for almost anyone — but only for the pure** (she learned the method from bearing and witnessing her son; she has handed out for 300 years the thing he achieved alone). She has withheld it from nearly all, including the founders who became saints, because she saw what they'd do with it — her refusal is the reason the corrupt three never got the ultimate power. A **Pure Resonant** is one for whom it unlocks *cleanly* (as Pure Primal rather than something corrupted) — which is why her sealed door filters for Pure. **Primal is morally neutral:** the son's was protective; the same power lit by vengeance or obsession is its mirror.

**Reading the shape:** the ladder measures how deep and how *clean* the bond runs. The one fork (Pure vs. Impure) is about the *purity* of the connection, and it echoes at the top — a Primal reached through a Pure bond vs. an Impure one is the seat of endgame divergence (branching paths / endings). Pure Alignment is where the misfit's *ascent* peaks; Primal is where his *self* is decided — not as good or evil, but as *what he resonates toward, and how cleanly.*
