# Story & Structure v0.1 — [WORKING TITLE]

*The game's spine, mapped into acts. This covers the **front half** (Prologue → the Da'Charta); the back half (the village, Karatsul, the saints, the Pure/Impure ending) comes next. Companion to the Core System and Cast docs. Choices I proposed are flagged **[call]** for you to confirm or veto.*

## Act 0 — Prologue: *What the War Takes*

**Function:** establish Siren, his Tide grief, the draft-machine as his personal enemy, Hymerdom in the home, and survivor's guilt — before he's anyone.

- A **fragmentary early memory**: Siren as a small child; his **father**, a conscript, leaves for the front and never returns. Siren is too young to hold it — so it's shown to *the player* as a haze the adult Siren himself can't access. **[call: dramatic irony — we see what he can't remember.]**
- Years later, sharp and vivid: his **older brother is drafted and taken.** This one Siren remembers — the goodbye is the wound the whole game hangs on.
- Siren, of age, **expects to be drafted too — and is rejected** ("physical attributes"). Survivor's guilt lands: the one left behind, spared for reasons he doesn't understand.
- Close on Siren in the family's poverty, **Hymerdom present but dormant**, grief already the water he moves through. His **mother is alive and still loving — but a *shell***, hollowed by losing her husband and elder son to the same machine. He comes home to someone present and half-gone; he protects a mother already partly lost. (Hollowing as a motif — grief hollows, the state hollows, the Artificial process hollows.)

## Act 1 — Present Day: *The Reason Wakes*

**Function:** inciting incident; reveal his rare Pure nature (which he doesn't grasp); mirror the First Resonant; get him into the Academy.

- Siren, older, in the poor district. A **bandit raid** (or some third-party attack — deliberately *not* plot-important) endangers his mother and neighbors. Cornered, *protecting them*, **Hymerdom awakens**: his Pure Resonance ignites for the first time, the gauntlet-blade wrapping his arm, a manifestation of Tide. The threat is incidental; the point is the awakening — and it deliberately mirrors the First Resonant, whose power woke defending the ones he loved.
- It's **witnessed.** The Academy claims all Resonants; he's swept up and inducted into the **Anoridami Resonant Academy** — pulled out of his world. Bittersweet: an "honor" and an escape from poverty, delivered by the same machine that ate his family.

---

## Act 2 — The Academy: *Found in a Hostile Place*

**Function:** the found-family core coalesces; establish the Academy's hostility, Siren's coveted purity, and the sibling dynamic.

- Siren enters low-born among highborn — **scorned, hazed, attacked** by students who resent the slum kid (many of whom are themselves Artificial or weak — the sneering is hollow).
- He meets **Earl** — the one kid with no cruelty in him (all smile-and-wave). At some point **Siren protects Earl** from bullies.
- **Cinne attacks Siren**, having misread the scene as *him* bullying her little brother — bitterly ironic, since he was Earl's defender. In the fight, Cinne — preternaturally perceptive — **feels that Siren is a true, Pure Resonant**, the real thing in a hall of forgeries. The misunderstanding breaks; her hostility turns to fascination and alliance. **The trio forms.**
- Threads planted: Cinne's secret disillusionment and truth-hunt; Earl's hollow sweetness; the powerful eyes already turning toward the coveted pure boy.

---

## Act 3 — Missions & the Cracks: *Serving the Lie*

**Function:** uncover the lie through escalating "assignments," and set the trap that will free him.

- **Framing — Academy missions run like *group-project assignments.*** You **assemble a team** and go. **Earl joins because Siren *asks* him** — an act of quiet inclusion toward the kid no one else picks. (The banal school-assignment framing making atrocities routine is part of the horror.)
- **The three missions — an *erosion* of faith** (each strips a layer, from a source Siren can't dismiss; Aurelian's grooming pulls the *opposite* way, so he's drawn toward belief and doubt at once):

  **① "The Mercy" — bandits, no mercy.** *(Faith intact.)* The party's first real deployment: clear a bandit stronghold preying on the poor. They win — but the state's protocol is **no mercy** (surrendered men executed; a village that "harbored" them razed by a Korveth officer who *enjoys* it). Siren, protective by nature, flinches — but bandits *did* prey on the weak, so he **rationalizes it** ("they had it coming"). First crack, suppressed. **The rot he glimpses:** a "bandit" turns out to be a starving draft-dodger, not a monster. **Aurelian** hears of the pure prodigy and reaches out — warm, flattering, *"you're wasted among the rabble."* Siren, starved for recognition, is moved. **Character:** Siren + Earl click (the kid nobody picked, picked); Cinne watches, assessing whether Siren's *real.*

  **② "Defectors" — hunt the resistance / Academy defectors.** *(Faith cracking.)* Greyer: these are "traitors" — but they're afraid, not evil, and they *know things.* Siren corners a fled Academy Resonant who, before he's taken, says: *"Do you even know what they made you? What this place is?"* Maybe Siren **spares one against orders** — his first small defiance. It haunts him. **The reveal:** Cinne, seeing his discomfort, decides to *test* him — the **long, pivotal chat**, where she lays out what she perceives: the **Artificials**, the disappearances, that most "Resonants" are fake, that the Academy is hollow. (She holds back the part about Earl.) Siren can't un-hear it. **Aurelian** deepens the grooming — offers a place at his side, glimpses of his **inner circle** (the turned cadre — *too* devoted, hollow-eyed; something's wrong). **Character:** Siren + Cinne become true allies; Earl's oblivious sweetness darkens by contrast.

  **③ "The Enemy" — the foreign front.** *(Faith broken.)* Deployed against the Outerlands foe — the "invaders" the Church has fought since the founding. But they're **people**, defending their homes; **Anoridami is the aggressor.** Siren learns (a captured soldier; a document; his own eyes) that the enemy narrative is **propaganda** — the founding "invasion" and this war are both lies. *Who are we really fighting, and why?* His faith in the cause **shatters.** **Aurelian**, sensing the doubt (and coveting the power), moves to **"pacify the heathens"** (Act 4) — the loyalty test / quiet removal; his charm now reads, to Siren and the player, as *manipulation.* **Character:** the group is bonded and disillusioned together — primed to turn.

- **The through-line:** truth reaches Siren from *three sources he can't wave off* — his own eyes (state cruelty), a dying defector + **Cinne's revelation**, and the enemy's humanity + the war-lie. And all of it runs *under* Aurelian's warm hand on his shoulder — the benefactor who is the villain, grooming the boy who'll end him.
- **The Public Face's loyal cadre.** As Siren **rises fast**, **Aurelian Aurent** takes personal interest (the other Saints can't be bothered with "a child"). He keeps a circle of **fanatically loyal Resonants he has "turned"** — living proof his method works (the same one he's running on Siren). **Siren fights them** and sees, each time, *who he'd have become if the grooming had taken.*
- **The Saints move him** — cold calculation dressed as honor: the pacification order is a **loyalty test** *and* a **quiet removal**, undone by their **ideological blind spot** (their contempt for the heathens can't imagine the Da'Charta hold the truth). The **found family deploys together** (Cinne engineers it; Earl because Siren asked).

## Act 4 — The Pacification & The Turn *(the Da'Charta)*

**Function:** the hinge of the whole game — the mission meant to test-and-remove Siren instead hands him his companion, the truth, and his brother. He switches sides.

- Siren's force moves against the frontier. **He fights Katariña** — that's how they meet: as enemies. But mid-clash she **recognizes him** — the pure stranger of Da'Charta prophecy — and stays her hand. The "savages" he was sent to crush are nothing he was told; the Da'Charta **feel his purity and welcome him**, and reveal what Resonance truly *is* (spirit itself; the redemption they foresaw). The lie he'd been assembling snaps into full focus. Note: the **Da'Charta keep *perfect peace*** and regard the anti-government **resistance as vigilantes** — the two are **adjacent, not allied**, both on the frontier but representing opposite answers to the empire (outlast it vs. fight it). Siren will stand between his companion (peace) and his blood (the knife).
- **The brother.** Siren discovers his lost brother **alive** — a **former Artificial who broke the state's control and turned rebel.** He presents as a **criminal** (survival cover) but is a **resistance fighter** underneath, and Siren's orders are to **put him down.** The gut of it: the brother was **super loyal at first**, so Siren — who last knew that loyal boy — **can't comprehend** the rebel in front of him (he's meeting his own future in his brother's past). The brother is **calm, understanding**; Siren reels. *(He's Earl's hopeful arc made literal — an Artificial who became real enough to rebel — which reopens the Silence question: has his reclaimed will grown a bond real enough to survive it?)*
- **The break.** Siren refuses — he **rescues his brother, breaks him out**, and turns on the mission. Full defection. He's a fugitive now, but he's found the truth, his companion (**Katariña joins**), and his brother. **[open: does the brother join the party? his name?]**
- After the Academy's scorn and the war-machine's theft, this is the first place Siren is simply *seen* — it lands like a warm bath. Reprieve, growth, the old ways.
- **⭐ The Maelor moment.** Among the Da'Charta, Siren learns **ancestor-communion** (the spirit-echoes the Church denies) and reaches the echo of **Maelor, the father whose face he can't remember.** Tide is loss and memory — here loss becomes *reunion.* A pure-emotion scene (no rules-lawyering the echoes). Then the road points toward the sealed village, Karatsul, and Primal.

## Act 5 — The Sealed Origin: *Karatsul*

**Function:** the truth's center; the emotional peak of the game; Primal + the Silence Reason.

- The party turns toward the **Resonant Temple** — the holy facade. The **Keeper Saint** guards it; breaching the facade is the act's obstacle. Da'Charta lore/prophecy guides them to the true village within.
- Only **Siren** (Pure, with Primal potential) can open the **sealed door.** He passes through — into the original village and **Karatsul's 300-year vigil.**
- **The guardian trial (the shadow-self).** Before the reward, the test: a **guardian that wears Siren's own face and fights as *the opposite of himself*** — his Impure mirror, grief hardened to wrath. **Karatsul calls it her son** (it wears the First Resonant's guardian-role, and her son and Siren are the same mirror of loss). This *is* the **Silence/Primal test embodied** — stripped to isolation, facing only yourself, *what survives?* The mother watches the boy-who-could-be-her-son battle the shadow of his own grief. **[design: the guardian is the *trial/gate*; the talk below is the *reward* — they amplify each other, they don't compete.]**
- **The talk, not the trial.** Karatsul isn't a boss or a sage dispensing power — she's a lonely, grief-frozen mother who wants, after three centuries, simply to be *talked to.* Siren — Tide, made of loss, the almost-image of her mute son — is the one person who can meet her there. Through *connection, not combat*, she opens the full truth: her son the First Resonant, the experiments, Primal, Silence, the origin of the Artificial program and the founding lie.
- Because he is pure and worthy, she **unlocks his Primal** and entrusts him the son's **Silence Reason.** The deepest power in the world passes hand-to-hand through simple human presence — the thesis of the whole game in one scene.
- **[call: Karatsul's fate — does she finally *rest / pass*, able to let go now that the truth and her son's Reason are carried on? A devastating release.]**

## Act 6 — The Detonation: *the Lie Breaks*

**Function:** the founding lie exposed; the saints confronted; the redemption begins.

- Armed with the truth and Primal, Siren and the party strike at the empire's heart. The **founding lie is detonated** — the saints are frauds/name-bearers, the venerated First Resonant was a tortured, disabled child, the Artificial program and sham democracy laid bare. The **scarcity lie collapses**: Primal potential is *universal*, so the entire hierarchy rests on a falsehood.
- The saints respond. **The Iron Hand (Artificial)** — immense power, but under Silence he is **annihilated** (nothing underneath). **The Keeper (Impure)** — *survives* Silence, but is corrupt and self-consuming: a harder, sadder fight. The **war/draft/Artificial machine** is broken or turned.
- **Character climaxes cluster here:** **Cinne's** Rage/Umbra crests — *does she master it or fall to Impure?* **Earl** may **bind a real element and become *real*** — the found family completing him, echoing the brother. The **brother's** fate and the **Silence question** resolve.
- **[call: how public is the detonation — a broadcast to the city, the saints collapsing from within, a popular uprising as the scarcity lie breaks?]**

## Act 7 — The Ending: *What Survives*

**Function:** the final confrontation and the Pure/Impure fork.

- The **Public Face** stands revealed as the **Perfect-evil apex villain** — the benefactor betrayal complete. His flawless bond to an **evil Reason** means **Silence does nothing** to him and he has **none of Impure's cracks**; Siren's own Perfect ("What Survives") **isn't enough.**
- Siren reaches **Primal** — **dual-wielding Hymerdom + the Silence Reason**: the founding's two purest strands (the loyal line + the origin itself) reuniting to finish what the Split interrupted, 300 years late.
- **The fork — determined by *how you played*, not a last-second menu pick.** The cumulative weight of your choices sets Siren's trajectory (Pure or Impure), and that changes **the final boss fight *itself* and the dialogue**, not just the closing cinematic. Pure-Siren and Impure-Siren confront the Public Face as *different people*, and he **speaks to each of them differently.**
  - **Pure ending — the redemption of Resonance:** protection over conquest, the origin restored, the prophecy fulfilled. He beats a Perfect-evil not by becoming a greater monster but by being the *truest bond there is.* Karatsul's vigil is answered. **He never takes power** — he comes through *whole and warm*, still a **kind man who helps people.** He **settles with the Da'Charta** (their knowledge fascinates him, and being Pure he can *hear and listen to Spirit* almost at their level with practice), with **Katariña** — his foretold love — beside him. *The grief-boy, finally seen and finally home.*
    > **The final scene.** When the fighting's done and Siren speaks of going back to his mother, Katariña smiles: *"That house is empty, but a new one is not."* (His mother is safe among the Da'Charta; a new home waits.) Later, the two sit on a great cliff over Da'Charta lands, looking toward the **Outerlands.** Katariña: *"It was prophesied that I would fall in love with the next Primal Resonant."* Siren, glancing around nervously: *"Uh… what else was prophesied?"* She stands, smiling — *"Much. Come."* — and extends her hand — *"I'll show you."* He takes it, smiles, and they walk back toward the village. **Credits.** (The Outerlands on the horizon = the world's next chapter.)
  - **Impure ending — the costlier victory, and the *same scene inverted*:** he still wins, but grief has curdled to vengeance — **cynical, closed off, refusing to help anyone.** The prophecy holds — Katariña *still* loves him, still stands at that cliff, still extends her hand — but this Siren **can't take it.** He's already turning away, or gone. The foretold love is real; he's just no longer a man who can reach back. (This is where Karatsul was *stuck* for 300 years.)
  - *Neither Siren becomes a god-king; the state deifies Resonance, and he holds the truest Resonance there is — yet he refuses the throne and the altar both. The real stakes of the fork are his **soul**, not his power: does he learn Karatsul's lesson (back to warmth) or repeat her curse (lost to bitterness)?*
- Resolution of threads: the **mother**, the **brother**, the **found family**, the **Da'Charta**, and the fate of **Anoridami**.
- **⭐ Stinger (the sequel hook).** When Aurelian falls, his Reason **Semaphore** glows — and the **physical form of Gesherimi** takes shape: about **20, scarred**, freed after 300 years bound. She scowls: ***"This isn't over. You would save only those you love — but I will save ALL RESONANTS."*** Then she fades, and the Reason is gone. She is Siren's dark mirror (save the loved few, Pure ⟷ save the many, Impure/by force), and the **Outerlands** — where Resonants are persecuted — are her cause and the world's next chapter. (Pairs with the Pure ending's cliff, gazing toward those same Outerlands: *"much else was prophesied."*)
- **[decided: the fork is the *sum of choices across the game* — it reshapes the final boss fight *and* its dialogue, not just the closing cinematic. Canonical/"true" ending TBD.]**
