## Ancient Origins — the Original Sin

**The old world.** Anciently, people lived much as the **Da'Charta do today** — tribal, deeply spiritual, largely peaceful. **Resonance was common to nearly everyone**, understood as **communion with the divine *or* the evil** (Pure/Impure was never a later invention — it's the ancient truth). Chieftaincy **rotated among the families every 10 years**, and required the chief to be a **Pure Resonant.**

**The fraud that started everything.** The **A'umai'enta** family had been skipped **twice** for lack of a Pure Resonant. So their leader, the great chief **Anaireneintai A'umai'enta**, **faked being Pure** to claim the chieftaincy — the **original sin the whole civilization fractally echoes** (Artificial Resonants, name-bearer saints, the hollow Academy: all the same lie, 300 years on). The **Charta** family **helped the farce out of pity.** *(The rot was midwifed by kindness.)*

**The birth of Impure.** Anaireneintai took into his court **Gesherimi**, a woman from beyond the tribes — the **Otherlands.** Pure Resonants felt her **umbral resonance** and dark intent and warned him; **stricken with her, he ignored them.** She slowly **polluted his Resonance from weak into *Impure*** — and *"it made him feel like he finally wasn't a farce anymore."* **He is the first Impure Resonant**, and that line is the seductive psychology of the whole Impure path: *what you take when you can't be Pure but can't bear being hollow.*

**The founding war (the false bottom).** Armies from the **Otherlands** came to **retrieve Gesherimi**; Anaireneintai **refused to give her up**, and a **devastating war** ensued. So the saints' official heroism — "we defeated the invaders" — conceals that **their own corrupt chief caused the war** (and the Otherlands had a just grievance). The **arsonist mythologized as the firefighter.** The catastrophe forced the **six families of powerful Resonance to rally as one for the first time** — the seed of Anoridami. *(Karatsul's mute son's unprecedented **Primal** ignites in this war defending his village — the true turning point later stolen and mythologized; see Core doc §9. Continuity note: **Pure existed anciently; the son's uniqueness was *Primal*, not mere Pure.**)*

## Cosmology & Spirit

- **Spirit** — one animating force suffusing all things. **Resonance is communion with Spirit** (Katariña resonates with it *directly*; most commune through a Reason). The ancient faith is fundamentally **animist**, and the **Da'Charta still keep it true.**
- **The moral axis is the *purity of the communion*, not the element.** What the ancients called *"communion with the divine or the evil"* is **Pure** (whole, undivided — "divine") vs. **Impure** (corrupt — "evil"). Good and evil live in *how whole or how corrupted the bond is*, never in which element you channel.
- **Elements are morally neutral** — the *colors* of Spirit. Some **lean** (Umbra toward the hidden/buried/grief — dark but **not bad**; Radiance toward the exposed/open), but a lean is a *temperament*, not a morality. A **Pure Umbra Resonant** is good — one who guards secrets and honors the dead with a whole heart. **No element is inherently evil.**
- Therefore **Gesherimi's evil was her *corruption* (Impure communion), not her umbral lean**, and an **evil Reason** is one with a *corrupted nature* baked in — element-agnostic.
- **The state co-opted the faith — the government *is* the Church** (see below).

## The Church of Resonance — the Government

Anoridami is a **theocracy**: the "government" is the **Church**, and the "three-party democracy" is the theocracy wearing a republic's clothes.

- **Monotheism — Resonance is God.** The one divine is **Resonance itself**, worshipped whole (a civilization that deifies its own magic/Spirit).
- **The three Saints are venerated keeper-demigods** — sub-divine "**Keepers of Resonance**," each heading a holy order that is also a branch of the state:
  - **Saint Aurelian Aurent, "the Beloved"** *(Public Face)* — the evangelizing, popular face of the faith; owns *narrative* (media, elections, love). Golden, charming; Siren's benefactor; the **Perfect-evil** villain, wed to an **evil Reason [working: "Semaphore"]** — a fitting name (a signaling device) for the man who controls the message; **possibly Gesherimi's vessel** (the founding corruption, bound and passed down the A'umai'enta/Aurent line).
  - **Saint Drau Korveth, "the Shield"** *(Iron Hand)* — the militant order, the church's sword; owns *force* (Academy, military, war, Artificial program). The **Artificial Saint.**
  - **Saint Thessa Sethran, "the Veiled"** *(Keeper)* — the temple priesthood; owns *soul* (religion, courts, the buried village). An austere woman; the **Impure** one.
- **The central martyr — the First Resonant.** The church venerates him as its holy martyr, his Temple the sacred center — **while the three Saints who "keep" him are the very ones who tortured him.** The faithful pray to the victim and his murderers at the same altar. The religion is a shrine built over a murder, by the murderers.
- **The sham "democracy"** = the three orders performing rivalry; the church-bloc rules as one no matter who "wins."
- **No sentient afterlife (doctrine).** Official teaching: at death your resonance returns to the great Resonance — no heaven, no persistence, you dissolve back into God. **But the truth contradicts it:** spirit *echoes.* Most dead leave **fading imprints** in Spirit; the honored or the strong-willed persist longer as **reachable echoes** (Da'Charta **ancestor-spirits**, the son's **guardian**); and rare, powerful wills abnormally **endure** almost whole (**Gesherimi**). The attuned can *commune* with these echoes — a truth the **Da'Charta keep** and the Church **buries** (a faith that admits the dead linger can't fully control them).
- **⭐ The Maelor moment.** Grief-defined, Tide-hearted Siren — who **cannot remember his father's face** — learns ancestor-communion among the Da'Charta and finally **reaches Maelor's echo.** Tide is loss and memory; here loss becomes *reunion.* A pure-emotion beat (spirit-echoes stay loose, no mechanics needed). *(Naming is mere Sr./Jr. convention — Maelor→Mael the namesake, younger kids named freely — no hidden meaning.)*

**✅ Gesherimi — the Half-Resonant (Siren's dark mirror).** Child of an **Outerland king and a Resonant woman** — **half-Resonant, half-royal.** Where the tribes revere Resonance, the **Outerlands fear Resonants as freaks** (the same gift, holy at home and monstrous abroad — see note below), so Gesherimi was **abused for her blood in her own palace**, half-sister to the legitimate **Princess**, a freak among royalty. She **escaped to the tribes** (where Resonants are free), arrived full of **vengeance**, and rallied followers against the kingdom that broke her — her **umbral resonance** the corruption the Pure Resonants sensed. She corrupted **Anaireneintai** (lover and instrument). Then the **king had a change of heart** — too-late remorse for her abuse — and sent armies to bring her home; Anaireneintai **refused**, and the founding war began. *The civilization is born from a father's guilt and a daughter's wound.*
