# Cast & Kits v0.1 — [WORKING TITLE]

*Companion to the Core System doc. This turns the cast into playable units: stat personality, role, and each character's Reason (with its Amplify signature and Awakened manifestation). All names in [brackets] are placeholders for you to claim. Numbers are first-pass and tunable — they exist to prove the kits feel distinct, not to be final.*

**Reading the stat blocks:** `STAT base (+growth/level)`. Base = Academy-entry (level 1). Growth is the "personality" — it's what makes the same combat system feel different in each pair of hands. Recall from the Core doc: `MaxHP = 40 + VIT×8 + level×6`, and SPR is the soul stat (fills Resonance, scales Amplified artes).

## The Starting Party (4 active)

Coverage check: a spirit-scaling protective **Vanguard** (MC), a weakness-exploiting **Artist** (Earl), a crit-burst **Duelist** (Cinne), and a durable, tradition-apart **Primal Warrior** (Katariña). Front-line/back-line, physical/arte, and standard-vs-old-ways are all represented.

### ⚔️ Siren — Tide Vanguard *(Pure Alignment)*
*Low-born heir of the loyal founding family; carries the heirloom Reason **Hymerdom**; holds unheard-of Pure Alignment he doesn't yet understand.*

**Element: Tide** — loss, memory, grief. He's the boy *defined by loss.* It surfaces obliquely: steady, quiet, dryly warm on the surface, grief running underneath — his protectiveness is *"I know what it is to lose, so I won't let you,"* not defiance. **Same element as Karatsul** — their final meeting is two people made of loss recognizing each other.

**The wound.** In Anoridami, **the lower class is conscripted** for the war effort. Siren's **elder brother, Mael, was drafted and lost.** His **father, Maelor, was taken the same way** when Siren was small — gone so early he can't even remember his face. (The house passes a name down the eldest line: **Maelor → Mael**; Siren, the youngest, is named *off*-tradition — by whom, and why, is a quiet open thread.) Siren *expected to be drafted too* but was **rejected on physical attributes** — spared, and left with the survivor's guilt of the one who didn't go. His grief and the world's rot are the same machine: the state that grinds his class into poverty also makes its people disappear. **But the sibling isn't dead** — he's alive: a **former Artificial who broke the state's control and turned rebel.** He was **super loyal at first** (like Cinne, like Siren himself — a believer who woke up), and now lives as a **criminal on the surface** (survival cover) with a **resistance fighter underneath.** Their reunion is a knife: Siren is **ordered to put him down**, can't reconcile this rebel with the loyal boy he remembers, and breaks — **rescuing him and defecting** (Act 4). He's **Earl's hopeful arc made literal** (a manufactured soul that reclaimed real will), which reopens the **Silence** question about him. *(Open: does he join the party? his name?)* *(Hook: "rejected for physical attributes" echoes the First Resonant, dismissed as a disabled child before he was the most powerful being alive — what did the draft board see and wave off?)*

**Stat block (L1)**
`VIT 14 (+3) · PWR 13 (+3) · FOC 8 (+1) · SPR 16 (+4) · GRD 12 (+2) · WRD 9 (+1) · AGI 11 (+2) · LCK 10 (+2)` → **HP 158**

**Role.** Front-line protective striker who guards by **control**, not tanking — Tide freezes threats and steals enemy tempo. Defining curve **SPR +4/level** (steepest in the party): since SPR scales both Resonance gain and Amplified power, his gauge and his signature pull further ahead every level. "Unheard-of Pure Alignment" as a number that quietly breaks the curve.

**His Reason — Hymerdom:** an ancient **gauntlet-sword** once wielded by his founding-warrior ancestor. As it attunes it **wraps around his arm** — the alignment ladder made visible on his body (a gripped blade early; fused to the arm at Pure Alignment). Tide-aligned; plain-looking, worth far more than the family knows.

*(Arte names aim at the register of "What Survives" — plain, loaded, specific — not element-plus-noun.)*
- **Basic — Attack** (unnamed): Tide, power 100.
- **Deadweight** (~6 MP): power 120, chance to **Freeze** (Stagger → drag the enemy's turn down). Core control — what the drowned become.
- **Breakwater** (~10 MP): +GRD/WRD to an ally + small SPR-scaled shield. The wall that takes the sea so you don't.
- **Full Fathom** (~14 MP, heavy): Tide AoE, power 90 to all; floods party Resonance on Ruptures. *(From "full fathom five thy father lies" — a drowning image pointing at his lost father.)*
- **Awakening (Tier I) — Watermark:** at Resonance 100 (cost decays with attunement → eventually free), spend for 3 turns — a tide rises at his feet and laps toward allies; weapon_atk ×1.5, allies take reduced damage while it holds. The line the flood leaves behind. Unlocks **Dirge** (heavy Tide hit + Freeze).
- **Amplify signature — "We Came For You":** dump full Resonance into one strike that crashes on the enemy *and* pulls back to shield the party. `power = 220 + SPR×2.5`; party barrier = `SPR×4` absorb. Offense + protection in one motion — the whole man. **Cinematic trigger:** the screen zooms to his eyes, he whispers *"We came for you…"*, everything flashes white, and it lands — the phrase is his ancestor's failed charge to save the Son, finally *succeeding* in his hands. (General rule: every Amplify gets a cinematic beat + a spoken cue — the game's all-out-attack flourish.)

**Feel:** the anchor. Each fight you decide whether to bank Resonance toward High Tide (party-wide protection) or spend it on a Requiem Tide burst. Scales from solid to terrifying.

> *SPR-scaling proof:* L10 (SPR 52) → Requiem Tide ≈ 175 neutral / **350 on a Rupture**, ~208 party shield. L20 (SPR 92) → ~450 on a Rupture, ~368 shield. Same button, roughly +30% for leveling alone — the prodigy curve.

**Arc:** already Pure (Tier 3), unfolding as story progresses. His **Perfect Alignment (Tier 4) is named "What Survives"** — the capstone of his grief-and-endurance arc: take everything from him, and the name of what he becomes at the peak is the answer to what's left. Late game, Karatsul unlocks his **Primal**, and he **dual-wields Hymerdom + the son's Reason** — the world's only **Silence** Reason, which returns to the world only in his hands (§ Core doc §5, §9).

### 🔮 Earl — Artist *(arte / weakness specialist)* — *Artificial*
*Siren's age and class. By all accounts a normal kid — until his parents had him made into an Artificial Resonant.*

**Who he is.** A **normal boy** from **new money** (a family risen from lower-middle-class to prominence), and **Cinne's younger brother.** To keep up appearances — they couldn't have one child a Resonant (Cinne) and the other not — his parents had him **transformed into an Artificial Resonant.** His spirit was strong enough to take the process, but **it never bound to an element** (hence elementless — his Attune mechanic is the manufactured boy *assigning* himself an element he doesn't truly have). Raised to be exactly what others needed, he's **all smile-and-wave, with almost no interior life of his own**, and **moot on most things** — not his fault; he's only ever done what he's told. **He doesn't really know he's Artificial, and doesn't much care** — he's at peace in the unknowing. Toward Cinne he's a **great little brother — no jealousy, he quietly adores her**, oblivious to the guilt she carries about him. **Open, hopeful arc:** the found family is the first place Earl gets to be a *person*; if he ever grows a genuine bond he could **bind a real element** and become the one Artificial who turns *real.*

**Stat block (L1)**
`VIT 9 (+1) · PWR 8 (+1) · FOC 15 (+4) · SPR 11 (+2) · GRD 8 (+1) · WRD 12 (+2) · AGI 14 (+3) · LCK 12 (+2)` → **HP 118**

**Role.** The party's **Rupture engine — a *setup* one, not a free one.** Capable artificial caster (high FOC + AGI), but his flexibility carries a real tempo cost. He's the economic heart when he lands Ruptures — cracking enemies open funds everyone's gauges — but he has to *call his shot* first. Fragile (lowest HP).

**The trade-off — Attunement.** Earl has **no fixed element**, and his base state is a genuine double-edge:
- **Unattuned (default):** all elements deal **1.0×** to him — he can *never* be Ruptured — **but he's a status magnet:** every element's affliction can land on him (Burn, Freeze, Seal, Weaken, all of it). Safe from spike damage, exposed to everything else.
- **Attune [element]** — a full action, no damage: sets Earl's active element to one of the six. It's a wind-up, not dead air — attuning also **fills his own Resonance gauge** a chunk. Now his artes carry that element (**can Rupture**) and his status exposure normalizes to that element's profile — but he accepts a **damage-weakness to the opposite element.** His element stays set until he switches.
- So every fight he's a live puzzle: *what do I become,* weighing my Ruptures, my new damage-weakness, and which statuses I'm exposing myself to. His loop is *scout → attune → unload* — he can devastate a known weakness but can't reactively hit every one for free. (This is the balance answer to the stress test.)

**His Reason — [catalyst, working name "Quill"]:** a focus/wand-blade.
- **Prism Lance:** his workhorse arte, cast in his *current* attuned element; on a Rupture, huge damage + refunds Resonance to allies. The team-arming move — once he's set up.
- **Amplify signature — [pending name]:** scales off FOC; a massive burst in his attuned element (or a rare all-element release). Emergent from Earl × catalyst.
- **Awakened manifestation:** floating geometric sigils of shifting color orbit him as he casts.

**Feel:** the sniper. Predict the weakness, spend a beat to load the right element, then blow the enemy open. High skill ceiling; punishes flailing, rewards planning. *(Alt option if you want him less punishing: give him one free "base" element he's born to, and only charge the Attune cost to leave it.)*

**🤝 Combo — "Calm Down" (Earl → Cinne):** his one genuinely *willed* act — reaching his big sister to vent her Rage before she Seizes. The hollow boy's truest moment. (Full spec in the Combo Moves roster.)

### 🗡️ Cinne — Duelist *(crit-burst)* — "SEEN"
*Two Academy classes above Siren and Earl. Poised, magnetic, lethal — the poster-child overachiever everyone watches and no one wants to spar.*

**Who she is.** **Highborn**, from **new money** (a family risen from lower-middle-class to prominence). By the Academy's standards a poster-child overachiever, and once **wildly devoted.** Two things broke that devotion into a secret: she is **preternaturally perceptive** — she began **detecting Artificial Resonants on her own**, seeing the lie no one else could — and it was confirmed in the cruelest way when her own **little brother, Earl, was made Artificial** by their parents. Now the devotion is a mask over a quiet, relentless hunt for **truth.** She and **Siren share the exact wound** — a sibling turned Artificial — the ground of their bond. With Earl the love is *agonized*: **he adores her, weightlessly, not knowing (or caring) what was done to him; she can't look at him without seeing the crime,** and carries it alone so he never has to.

**The thorn (her gauge — Rage).** Her emerging **Umbra** isn't a wound — it's an *appetite* ("a thorn of the flesh"). Cinne genuinely **enjoys violence** and is deeply **rebellious**, caged for years to be what was expected. Mechanically this is her personal gauge: **Rage**, fed by violence (crits landed, hits taken), which **isn't spent — it's survived.** At max it **seizes her** into a bloodthirsty, sadistic state (her dark later Amplify): massive crit output, but she loses control — indiscriminate, unreinable, for a few turns. Her arc isn't "will she be saved," it's **"will she master it or will it master her?"** — a live **Impure risk from the inside**, watched as a bar all fight.

**Her heart — Siren's brother.** Cinne's love interest is **Siren's older brother** (the reformed Artificial rebel), *not* Siren — and it's thematically load-bearing, not a side plot. She's the girl who *perceives* Artificials, whose own little brother (Earl) was hollowed into one; falling for **an Artificial who broke free and became *real*** means she loves the living proof that what was done to Earl can be *undone.* (Ages line up too — both are older than Siren/Earl.)

**Element: Gale (to start) → gains Umbra later.** Gale (freedom, the untouchable) is her surface *and* her speed/multi-hit identity; as her buried self surfaces, **Umbra bleeds in** — her element evolution *is* her character reveal.

**Stat block (L1)**
`VIT 11 (+2) · PWR 14 (+3) · FOC 9 (+1) · SPR 12 (+2) · GRD 8 (+1) · WRD 8 (+1) · AGI 16 (+4) · LCK 15 (+3)` → **HP 134**

**Role.** The tempo monster and the party's high-variance nuke. **AGI +4** = she acts more often than anyone (CTB rewards this directly); **LCK +3** stacks crit (`5% + LCK/2`). Where Siren is reliable, Cinne is a *gamble with a huge ceiling* — glass (low GRD/WRD), fastest thing on the field, deletes weakened targets before they act. She's the mechanical proof of "different soul, different beast": her signature scales off her **crit build (AGI/LCK/PWR)**, not spirit.

**Her Reason — Fleshound** (Flesh + Hound): a **set of daggers** that move like a *pack* — predatory, relentless, running the target down and biting. Not an elegant duelist's blade; a hunt.
- **Basic — Attack** (Gale, power 100, *fast action* — low tick cost).
- **Needlework** (~6 MP): 2–3 fast hits, each rolls crit independently. Her bread-and-butter chip that fishes for crits.
- **Overtake** (~8 MP, fast): sharply reduces her *next* action's tick cost — she cuts in line and acts again almost immediately. Tempo theft; the hound closing distance.
- **Opening** (~7 MP): marks a target — the next hit on it is a **guaranteed crit** and ignores some GRD. Her setup for a burst (or to hand a crit to an ally). The pack finds the throat.
- **Awakening (Tier I) — Split Second:** the daggers scatter into a spectral *pack* — she and her blades blur into three places at once. For 3 turns her crit chance jumps and Overtake-tempo is baked in.
- **Amplify — "Last Dance"** *(cue: "Try to keep up.")*: the whole pack descends — a storm of strikes, each rolling crit at a boosted rate. `hits = 4 + floor(AGI/15)`; each hit power 70; crit chance during it = `base + 40%`. Scales off AGI (more hits), LCK (more crits), PWR (per-hit).

**⚙️ Rage — full spec (0–100). A *push-your-luck damage bar.*** Where Siren *chooses* to spend Resonance, Cinne mostly **rides** hers.
- **Higher Rage = more damage.** Cinne deals **+0.5% damage per point of Rage** (~**+40–50%** near max) — so you *want* to run her hot. Venting is a real *sacrifice*: you give up the bonus to stay safe.
- **Fills fast, from violence:** crit landed **+12** · normal hit **+4** · hit taken **+8** · passive **+3/turn.** Her crit-heavy kit feeds her own danger — the better she fights, the closer she is to the edge (~L10: **~4 turns to max** unmanaged).
- **Seized at 100 → "Bloodrun" (3 turns) — you *lose her.*** The player loses **all** control: she auto-attacks the nearest/random target (allies *not* safe), and **you cannot target her with any items, potions, heals, or buffs** — she's cut off from all support. Upside: **crit +50%, PWR/AGI up, acts every turn.** Downside is *agency*: if she's hurt when she Seizes, you can only watch — a low-HP Cinne can swing wildly and *die* with your healer standing helpless. Ends with Rage → 0 and a brief comedown (AGI/accuracy dip, 1 turn).
- **The loop:** ride Rage as high as you dare for the damage, then vent *just* before it seizes. Two vent tools: her own **Last Dance (−50)**, and her brother **Earl** — but note **Earl's "Calm Down" is *prevention, not rescue*:** he can pull her back before she goes over, but **once she's in Bloodrun even he can't reach her** until it passes. There's a line past which the one person who keeps her human can't follow.
- **Arc, made playable:** as her **Umbra** surfaces, Rage fills faster / Bloodrun bites harder — *master it or be mastered.* Late option: **choose** to trigger Bloodrun (weaponize the thing she fears).

**🤝 Combo — "Calm Down" (Earl → Cinne).** Earl reaches his big sister and pulls her back from the edge (vents her Rage). The moot boy's one genuinely willed act — and **if Earl is out of the fight, Cinne loses her anchor and is far likelier to Seize.** Full spec in the Combo Moves roster below.

> *Crit-scaling proof (L10):* AGI 52 → **7 hits**; crit chance 26%+40% = **66%**. Per hit ≈ 43 expected → ~**298** average, but swingy: ~336 if every hit crits, ~224 if none do. Siren's burst is a certainty; hers is a *dice roll you're happy to make.*

**Feel:** chain her speed, set up with Opening, and either bank toward Split Second (a whole Awakened turn of crits) or gamble it all on Last Dance. Reliable Siren / volatile Cinne is the party's core rhythm contrast.

**Open (her inner life):** *why* is she at the Academy, and what's under the poise? Is she a true Resonant or — given "most are Artificial or weak" — something she's hiding? Does she clock Siren as the real thing before anyone else (a fellow genuine article), and is her interest mentorship, alliance, or her own agenda?

### 🪶 Katariña — the Amplifier *(the old ways)*
*A warrior of the **Da'Charta** — the tribe formed when the two exiled founding families merged with the indigenous people of the heathen provinces. Grounded, unbitter, apart. She descends from a founding line, kin to Siren across the divide.*

**Resonance: "Abnormal" (per the Academy) — she resonates with *spirit itself.*** Not a Reason-as-weapon, not one of the six elements — the substrate everyone else only touches secondhand. The Academy files it as a defect because it's the **old, truer form they've lost**; she's living proof they don't understand their own subject. Practically: **no elemental affinity** (can't be Ruptured on the wheel — defensively stable), and because she reaches the spirit *in others*, her power is **communal** — she lifts the party. The exact inverse of the empire's private, weaponized Resonance.

**Stat block (L1)**
`VIT 17 (+4) · PWR 13 (+2) · FOC 11 (+2) · SPR 16 (+4) · GRD 15 (+3) · WRD 12 (+2) · AGI 8 (+1) · LCK 10 (+2)` → **HP 182**

**Role — the wall *and* the wellspring.** Tankiest in the party (highest VIT/GRD), slowest (low AGI), high SPR. She's a **durable support-amplifier**: her own damage is secondary; her value is making Siren, Cinne, and Earl hit harder, fill faster, and endure. Mechanically **a different tradition, not another Academy kit.**

**Her gauge — Fervor** (her personal form of Resonance, §Core doc). Builds by *enduring* (taking hits) and by resonating with the party's spirit (allies acting / landing Ruptures feed it). Spent on **old-ways invocations**, not a weapon Awakening:
- **Rootbind** — plant a totem: the party regenerates HP each turn while it stands.
- **Ancestral Roar** — push back all enemies' CTB counters (tempo denial) + lower their GRD.
- **Old Blood** — her own defense-piercing strike (her main personal damage; ignores a chunk of GRD).
- **Kindle** *(the amplifier core)* — pour spirit into a chosen ally: instantly **fill a large chunk of their gauge** and empower their next Amplify. She *arms* the team's biggest moments.

**Her Reason — the Staff of Nagandahl:** an ancestral, half-living staff named for **Nagandahl**, a patriarch of one of the two families that left — the Da'Charta counterpart to Siren's Hymerdom. Old-ways relic, not a manufactured Reason.

**Her "Amplify" — [The Da'Charta Stand]:** rather than a personal burst, she opens the spirit-channel and the *ancestors manifest* — a party-wide amplification: everyone's gauge surges and their next Amplify is empowered. Cinematic cue: the totems bloom and the dead stand behind the living.

**⚙️ Fervor — full spec (her gauge, 0–100).** The opposite of Rage: **slow, communal, adversity-fed** — you build it *for the team.*
- **Fills from endurance & the party's struggle:** hit taken **+10** · passive **+SPR/10** (~+5 at L10) · an ally lands a Rupture **+6** (she resonates with their spirit) · an ally drops to critical/downed **+15** (she rises in adversity). She's the tank, so she soaks hits and outlasts. (~L10: ~+15–20 per active turn.)
- **Invocations (spend Fervor):**
  - **Rootbind (25):** totem — party regen ~8% max HP/turn, 3 turns.
  - **Ancestral Roar (35):** push *all* enemies' CTB back + −GRD to all, 3 turns.
  - **Old Blood (30):** her defense-piercing strike — power 180, ignores 50% GRD (her main personal damage).
  - **Kindle (40):** pour spirit into one ally — instantly **+40 to their gauge** (Resonance/Rage) *and* **+30% to their next Amplify.** The amplifier core.
- **The Da'Charta Stand (needs full 100):** party-wide — **+50 gauge to everyone**, **+25% to each ally's next Amplify**, and a **party regen aura (2 turns).** She arms the team's biggest coordinated moment. No standard Awakening (old ways); her manifestation is the ancestor-totems.

**Feel:** the durable heart that turns the enemy's aggression into party sustain and turns her allies' good turns into *great* ones. Where the Academy kits chase the bright loud spike, she's endurance and lift. Her presence is a living argument that the refined tradition lost something — and a bridge: when she teaches Siren the old way, the two systems begin to reconcile.

**Why she leaves.** The Da'Charta **elders foresaw it** — they foresaw the **redemption of Resonance**, and told Katariña she would **accompany the stranger who would come.** She senses it herself, too. And when the stranger — **Siren** — arrives, **no one protests, because they all *feel* it: Siren is pure.** Where the empire is blind to what he is (or hungers to exploit it), the people who resonate with spirit itself recognize his purity on sight — the first place in his life he is simply *seen* and welcomed.

**Her heart — Siren (foretold).** The prophecy named her at *birth*: she would **fall in love with the next Primal Resonant, and he with her** — her very existence a marker that the redemption was near, and (once they meet Siren) the sign that tips the Da'Charta to **shelter his mother.** A **slow burn**, unlike Cinne + Mael — foretold, but earned across the journey until it's *hers*, not fate's. In the Pure ending she and Siren **settle among the Da'Charta** (he can *hear Spirit* almost at their level, being Pure) — the grief-boy finally home. *(She's carried the prophecy of her own heart her whole life, watching for him — which is why the slow burn matters: it becomes a choice, not a decree.)*

### ⛓️ Mael — the Liberator *(Defiant Bruiser)* — *Artificial (broke free)*
*Siren's elder brother. Conscripted, forged Artificial, once a true believer — then he broke the state's control and became a rebel. Criminal on the surface (survival), resistance underneath. Calm, understanding, at peace with himself in a way Siren isn't. **Cinne's love.** Joins in Act 4 after Siren, ordered to put him down, rescues him instead.*

**Who he is.** The eldest Seir son (named down the line: Maelor → Mael). Drafted young, **forged into an Artificial Resonant**, and — like Cinne, like Siren — **super loyal at first.** Then he woke up, broke his bindings, and turned on his makers. He's **Earl's arc made literal**: a manufactured soul that reclaimed real will. Where Earl is still hollow-sweet, Mael is *whole* — he found himself. That's why Siren "can't comprehend" him at first: he's meeting the loyal boy he mourned, remade into a free man.

**Element: Tide (the maelstrom) → awakens Gale (the tempest).** Native **Tide**, same element as Siren but **opposite soul** — Siren is the tide that *shelters and grieves*; Mael is the **maelstrom** that *breaks and drowns.* And he's an **awakened-dual** (§ Core doc §5): he grows **Gale** (freedom — the storm he *seized* when he broke his chains), becoming a full **tempest**, sea and storm. Born to the water, grew the wind. His having a real, bound element at all (vs. Earl's elementlessness) is quiet proof he *became real.*

**Stat block (L1)**
`VIT 15 (+3) · PWR 15 (+3) · FOC 9 (+1) · SPR 12 (+2) · GRD 13 (+3) · WRD 10 (+2) · AGI 10 (+2) · LCK 11 (+2)` → **HP 166**

**Role — the Liberator.** A sturdy, aggressive mid-liner who **breaks control.** The Artificial who snapped his own chains now snaps everyone's — cleansing debuffs, freeing allies from status, turning adversity into power. The party's answer to enemy control (stuns, seals, freezes) and a defiant bruiser in his own right.

**His gauge — Defiance (Spend-family, flavored).** Reuses Siren's *you-choose-when* archetype, but it fills from **adversity**, not success:
- **Fills:** hit taken **+8** · a debuff/status landed on him **+10** · per enemy action while outnumbered **+2** · an ally downed **+10.** He rises as things go wrong.
- **Break (30):** cleanse all his own debuffs, gain **control-immunity** (no stun/seal/freeze) 2 turns, +25% PWR 2 turns.
- **Unshackle (25):** cleanse one **ally** of all debuffs/control. *(Cannot touch Cinne's Bloodrun — that stays unreachable, prevention-only.)*
- **Amplify — [working name] cue: *"It was all… a lie!"*:** a defiant Tide/Gale **tempest** burst, `power = 210 + PWR×2`. Not cocky defiance — the *anguished cry of a betrayed true believer*, everything he gave to the lie turned back on it.
- **Awakening (Tier I):** a churning maelstrom rises around him — weapon_atk ×1.5, and enemies near him have their control effects *slip* (reduced status accuracy). The storm that won't be held.

**His Reason — Heanmetal:** a chain-and-blade **forged from the very manacles that bound him** as an Artificial — broken and turned on his makers. Rough, industrial, *metal.* Not an ancestral relic (Siren carries Hymerdom); a weapon he *made from what enslaved him.*

**⭐ The Silence question (his defining stake).** Artificial bonds are hollow — **Silence annihilates them.** But Mael **reclaimed real will.** So: is his self *real* enough to survive Silence, or does the deepest test still find the manufacture underneath? His whole arc hangs here — **proof, or heartbreak, about whether an Artificial can truly become real.** And **Earl's hope rides on the answer.** *(Open — a devastating thing to decide.)*

**Web.** Brother to **Siren**; love of **Cinne**; and to **Earl** — Cinne's Artificial little brother — he's living proof of what Earl could become. He sits at the center of the found family, tying all three others together.

## 🤝 Combo Moves

*Joint abilities between specific party members (à la dual-techs) — relationships expressed as mechanics. Require all participants active & able; unlock as bonds deepen (story-gated); cost the participants' gauge and/or a cooldown.*

1. **Friendstrike — Siren + Earl** · *"Earl!" — "Mmhm" 😊*
   The first friendship, and the warmest. A coordinated two-hit: Siren's Tide strike + Earl's arte in his *current attuned element*, same target. If either element hits the enemy's weakness → **guaranteed Rupture** (2× + big gauge to *both*). Cheap, reliable, early-unlock — the combo that says *Siren picked the kid no one else would.*

2. **Calm Down — Earl + Cinne** · *(Earl grounds his sister)*
   The Rage-vent. **Cinne's Rage −40**, Earl **+20 gauge.** **Prevention, not rescue** — useless once she's in Bloodrun. The anchor. *(If Earl is benched/downed, Cinne is far likelier to Seize.)*

Three distinct faces fronting a single unified bloc — and, emerging, a *taxonomy* of what a Resonant can be. (Note world-fact: **almost all Resonants are Artificial or weak true ones**, all just called "Resonants"; genuine powerful true Resonants are rare, which is why Siren is coveted as a potential *tool*.)

The perfect capstone enemy for that reveal.
3. **[The Keeper]** — guards the Temple facade and the secret of the village; enforces the burial of the truth. Open: likely the **Impure** true Resonant (power bought through corruption/sacrifice) — which would complete the trio as *artificial / true-corrupt / impure*, a living map of the alignment system's dark side.
